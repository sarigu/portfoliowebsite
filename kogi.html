3 part week submission

Task 03.02.01
BBasic Animation

Kogi's lunchbox adventures

about the task

The assignment was to create a whole concept for an animation. The design should be based on one of the styles the teachers presented.

What I did

The inspiration

Lately, I’ve been hearing a lot of stories about students getting mocked in school for bringing lunchboxes that contain „exotic“ food. I thought, it would be nice to create something that helps little kids getting familiar with different foods from around the world.

the design

I picked flat design. The style mainly focuses on simplicity. The style features clean and two-dimensional shapes made of big, solid colour blocks. One can also find open spaces and bright colours. The illustrations are influenced by lunchboxes and aim to get kids familiar with different foods in a playful way.

The characters

The backgrounds

the story

The user can choose from a broad range of different lunchboxes. Each lunchbox represents the most common food that is eaten for lunch in school/at work in a specific country. The main character (named Kogi) gets put into the chosen lunchbox either by a spoon, chopsticks or by hands (depending on how people eat in this country). In each of the lunchboxes Kogi experiences another small, animated adventure. This is the story for a „japanese“ lunchbox.


The animation

I drew the main character in different positions. I also created a spritesheet animation for the little tomato jumping.

What I have learned

As it was the first time we didn’t have to hand in every subtask individually I learned a lot about time management. I also exercised my drawing skills in Illustrator by creating characters and spritesheets. I considered animation principles, character sheets and dramaturgical elements when making decisions regarding the animation.
